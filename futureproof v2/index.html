A Different Way to Learn

Students learn by building real projects.

Instead of lectures and worksheets, students work on hands-on projects every week — designing, building, testing, and presenting their work.

Step 01
📖

Learn a New Concept

Each week starts with a new idea — coding, AI, business, or finance — grounded in real-world context.

Step 02
🛠️

Build a Project

Students immediately apply what they learned by creating something tangible they can show and use.

Step 03
🎤

Demo What You Made

Every Saturday, students present their work. The habit of finishing and showing builds real confidence.

Step 04
📈

Improve & Iterate

Feedback from peers and mentors helps students refine their work and deepen their understanding.

Curriculum

Four areas shaping the future.

Students explore the key domains that are defining how the world works — and how to build in it.

💻

Technology

Students learn how apps and digital tools are built. They create their own small apps and interactive projects to see how software actually works.

  • Personal website
  • Learning tracker or productivity app
  • Small game or interactive project
🤖

Artificial Intelligence

Students learn how modern AI tools work and how to use them to build helpful systems that automate tasks.

  • Simple AI assistant
  • Automated workflows
  • AI-powered tools for everyday use
🏪

Business & Entrepreneurship

Students learn how ideas turn into products and services. They explore simple business models and experiment with launching a small idea.

  • Business model exploration
  • Mini product or service launch
  • Understanding customers and value
₿

Money & Digital Finance

Students explore the basics of money and how digital systems like Bitcoin work — and why technology is changing finance.

  • How Bitcoin and digital money works
  • Basics of saving and value
  • Technology's role in payments

Program Structure

8 weeks of hands-on learning.

The program is divided into two levels — first building the foundation, then applying it in the real world.

Level 1 · Weeks 1–4

Building with Technology

Students learn the basics by creating simple apps and digital tools. Each week they build something new and present it.

  • Personal website
  • Learning tracker
  • Productivity app
  • Small game or interactive project
Level 2 · Weeks 5–8

Applying Technology in the Real World

Students expand their skills by exploring AI, business, and finance — bringing everything together in a final project.

  • AI tools and automation
  • Small business experiments
  • Bitcoin and digital finance
  • Sharing and marketing their ideas
